  1. Identify 4-5 user requirements for the system.

Answers:

  1. user requirements

    • Real-time monitoring systems informs the current status if the container (% of being full)

    • Integration with already existing systems

      • Systems allows for stopping the process of handling textile waste

      • Controlling the speed of the textile waste managment system

    • User friendly UI

2) You are an experienced Scrum specialist in your organization. A group of students come to visit your organization and do not know anything about Scrum.

Explain the Scrum events shortly to visiting students:

  1. Sprint Planning

  2. Daily Scrum

  3. Sprint Review

  4. Sprint Retrospective

Answer:

  • Sprint Planning = Planning of work over a short period of time.A plan defines the goals of the next sprint. It defines what can be done and how will the work be completed. During the planning, attendees discuss most important backlog items.

  • Daily Scrum = Short session for evaluation of progress. During the meeting , a plan for the next day of work is formed.

  • Sprint Review = A meeting where Scrum team and important stakeholders communicate. Includes: progress inspection and evaluation of sprint backlog. Reviews: what has been accomplished and what has changed.

  • Sprint Retrospective = A meeting between all members of Scrum. Maximum 3 h long. The goal of Sprint Retrospective is to increase quality and effectiveness. Discussion about what went well during the Sprint, problems and.

3) Compare Scrum approach to the traditional plan-based software project management approach regarding the following aspects:

  • how people are allocated to the project (e.g. compare roles between agile and plan-based approach, discuss how teams are organized)

  • how customers are involved in software development

  • how project transparency is ensured / not ensured in these approaches

Answer:

  • Compared to traditional, hierarchical pland-based project managment, Scrum teams are more flexible, team members may have multiple roles and switch tasks. Scrum teams are more self-organized and members may share tasks among team members.

  • Unlike in traditional plan-based teams, Scrum approach allows for active customer involment. Scrum approach encourages keeping connection with customers. In Scrum Product Owner has special position in different Scrum meetings. Traditionally customer involment is usually limited to preplanned project milestones and detailed documentation.

  • Backlogs add transparency in Scrum projects. Scrum projects also revisit these blacklogs quite often. Sprint reviews and daily scrum meetings ensure transparency between different roles and teams. Traditonal plan-based project managments mostly relies on documentation, which creates transparency in theory. Documentation isn’t very flexible and it propably wont cover changes in the project. Plan-based project managment doesen’t include consistant inspection of progress between different teams.
